Your Free Emergency Dentistry Checklist

Instant PDF-ready guide: What to do for any dental emergency — including pediatric trauma scenarios — plus local provider map and pain-management tips.

1. Severe toothache or throbbing pain✅ Rinse warm salt water • Call now • Do not apply heat
2. Knocked-out tooth (adult or child)✅ Keep in milk or saliva • See dentist in <60 min for reimplantation
3. Abscess or facial swelling with fever✅ Do not pop • Seek care immediately • Antibiotics may be needed
4. Broken, chipped, or fractured tooth✅ Save fragments • Rinse gently • Temporary filling if needed
5. Lost crown, filling, or veneer✅ Temporary cement or sugar-free gum • Book same-day repair
6. Uncontrolled bleeding after extraction✅ Bite on clean gauze 30–45 min • Call provider immediately
7. Pediatric emergency (child tooth trauma)✅ Stay calm • Save tooth • Pediatric specialist ready within 60 min
8. Jaw injury, dislocation, or facial trauma✅ Ice 10 min on / 10 min off • ER if breathing issue
9. Object stuck between teeth or gum✅ Floss gently • Do not force • Professional removal required
10. Loose implant or bridge✅ Avoid chewing on side • Implant specialist needed same-day
11. Sudden severe pain after root canal✅ Contact endodontist or emergency provider immediately
12. Wisdom tooth infection / pericoronitis✅ Salt water rinses • Urgent oral surgery consult
13. Avulsed or luxated permanent tooth✅ Replant if possible • Transport in milk • Time is critical
14. Allergic reaction to dental medication✅ Stop medication • Call 911 if swelling affects airway

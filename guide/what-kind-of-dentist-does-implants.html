What Kind of Dentist Does Implants?

Patients are often surprised that not every dentist places implants. The procedure requires extra surgical training and experience with bone and gum tissue.

Types of Dentists Who Perform Implants

  • • Oral and maxillofacial surgeons – highest surgical training for complex cases
  • • Periodontists – specialists in gums and bone, ideal for implant foundation
  • • Implant specialists – dentists who focus almost exclusively on implants
Quick tip:
Choose based on your case complexity. Straightforward single-tooth implants can often be done by a well-trained general dentist.

Can a General Dentist Do Implants?

Yes — many general dentists complete advanced implant training and place implants successfully every day. However, for multiple implants, bone grafts, or patients with medical complications, an oral surgeon or periodontist is best for the safest outcome. The main risks with an inexperienced general dentist include higher implant failure rates, nerve damage, sinus issues, or poor long-term integration.

🚨 When This Becomes a Dental Emergency

If an existing implant is loose, painful, or shows signs of infection (swelling, pus, fever), you need immediate care to protect surrounding bone and teeth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of dentist does implants?

Oral surgeons, periodontists, and specially trained general dentists place dental implants.

What type of dentist does dental implants?

Primarily oral surgeons and periodontists, though some general dentists with extra certification also offer the service.

Who does dental implants?

Specialists trained in surgical implant placement — oral surgeons and periodontists are the most common.

Can a general dentist do implants?

Yes, if they have completed advanced implant training. For complex cases, a specialist is usually recommended.

Is an oral surgeon better for dental implants?

Oral surgeons have the most extensive surgical training, making them ideal for complicated or multiple-implant cases.

Do periodontists do implants?

Yes — periodontists are gum-and-bone specialists and place implants routinely.

What is the difference between a general dentist and an implant specialist?

Implant specialists focus almost exclusively on implants and often have hundreds more procedures under their belt.

Can any dentist place dental implants?

No — proper surgical training and experience are required for safe, long-lasting results.

How long does a dental implant procedure take?

The actual placement usually takes 1–2 hours per implant, but the full process spans several months of healing.

Are dental implants done in one day?

What if my implant fails?

Contact an emergency dentist immediately — prompt care can often save the site for a replacement.

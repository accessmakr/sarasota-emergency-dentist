🚨 What Counts as a Dental Emergency?

LIFE-THREATENING (Call 911)
  • • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • • Swelling that blocks airway
  • • Facial bone trauma
URGENT (See Dentist Same-Day)
  • • Severe tooth pain / abscess
  • • Knocked-out or loose tooth
  • • Broken tooth with pain
  • • Infection with fever
NON-URGENT

Mild sensitivity, loose crown without pain, routine issues — schedule within 48 hours.

📜 Expanded ADA Guidelines Details (2026)

The American Dental Association (ADA) provides evidence-based protocols emphasizing rapid action, proper storage media, and antimicrobial stewardship. Key expanded recommendations include:

Tooth Avulsion (ADA Council on Scientific Affairs)
  • • Reimplantation within 30 minutes yields highest success rate (up to 90%)
  • • Preferred storage: Hank’s Balanced Salt Solution (HBSS); milk is acceptable second choice
  • • Do not scrub root or remove attached tissue
  • • Splinting and follow-up within 24–48 hours required
Infection & Pain Management
  • • Antibiotics ONLY for systemic signs (fever, swelling, malaise) — not routine
  • • First-line analgesics: ibuprofen or acetaminophen (ADA-endorsed dosing)
  • • Avoid placing aspirin directly on tissue
  • • Cold compress: 10 min on / 10 min off to reduce swelling
Bleeding & Trauma
  • • Direct pressure with gauze or moist tea bag for minimum 10–15 minutes
  • • No aspirin products (increases bleeding risk)
  • • Preserve tooth fragments in milk for possible bonding
General ADA Principles
  • • Time is critical — every minute counts for avulsed teeth
  • • Seek care from ADA-member dentists when possible
  • • Tele-dentistry triage is acceptable only for initial guidance

Immediate Action Checklists

🦷 Knocked-Out (Avulsed) Tooth

  1. Stay calm. Time is critical — best chance of saving tooth is within 30–60 minutes (ADA).
  2. Hold tooth by the crown (chewing surface) only — never touch the root.
  3. Gently rinse tooth with water or saline if dirty (do NOT scrub or remove tissue).
  4. If possible, gently re-insert into socket and hold with clean gauze.
  5. If re-insertion not possible: place in container of milk, saliva, or saline (never plain water long-term; HBSS preferred per ADA).
  6. Call or go to nearest listed Sarasota emergency dentist immediately.

ADA Seal of Acceptance tooth-preservation kits recommended if available.

🩸 Uncontrolled Bleeding

  1. Rinse mouth gently with warm salt water.
  2. Place clean gauze or tea bag over bleeding site and bite down firmly for 10–15 minutes (ADA protocol).
  3. Apply cold compress to outside of cheek (10 minutes on / 10 off).
  4. Do NOT use aspirin — it increases bleeding (use ibuprofen if no contraindications).
  5. If bleeding does not stop after 20 minutes → seek emergency dental care or ER.

😣 Severe Toothache or Abscess

  1. Rinse mouth with warm salt water to clean area.
  2. Gently floss to remove any trapped food.
  3. Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen (follow label; consult pharmacist if needed — ADA first-line).
  4. Apply cold compress to cheek for swelling.
  5. Do NOT place aspirin directly on gum.
  6. Contact Sarasota emergency dentist same-day — abscesses can spread quickly.

💥 Broken or Cracked Tooth

  1. Rinse mouth with warm water.
  2. Save any broken fragment in milk or damp gauze (ADA preservation guideline).
  3. Apply cold compress to reduce swelling/pain.
  4. If sharp edge cuts tongue/cheek, cover with dental wax or sugar-free gum.
  5. See dentist same-day to prevent infection.

👦 Pediatric Dental Emergencies (Children Under 12)

Children have unique needs: primary (baby) teeth and developing permanent teeth require different handling. Always seek pediatric dental care promptly. Never give aspirin to children due to Reye’s syndrome risk (ADA warning).

🦷 Knocked-Out Baby (Primary) Tooth

  1. Do NOT re-insert the baby tooth — it can damage the permanent tooth bud underneath (ADA guideline).
  2. Rinse child’s mouth gently with water; apply cold compress to cheek for comfort.
  3. Keep child calm and give age-appropriate pain relief (acetaminophen or ibuprofen — follow weight-based dosing).
  4. Bring child to a pediatric dentist or emergency clinic the same day to check for underlying damage.
  5. Save the tooth in milk if requested by dentist for evaluation.

🦷 Knocked-Out Permanent Tooth (Child/Teen)

  1. Follow adult avulsion protocol above — time is even more critical in growing children.
  2. Attempt gentle re-insertion if child is cooperative and tooth is clean.
  3. If not possible, store in milk or HBSS and seek care immediately.
  4. Call pediatric dentist or 24/7 line right away — permanent teeth in children have high re-implantation success when treated fast.

😣 Severe Toothache, Abscess, or Facial Swelling in Children

  1. Rinse mouth gently with warm salt water.
  2. Give child-safe pain medication (acetaminophen or ibuprofen — never aspirin).
  3. Apply cold compress to cheek (10 min on/off).
  4. Monitor for fever, difficulty swallowing, or breathing — call 911 if present.
  5. See pediatric dentist same-day — infections spread faster in children (ADA emphasis).

Your free emergency dental checklist

Instant PDF guide: What to do for any dental emergency — including pediatric scenarios — plus local provider map.

1. Severe toothache or throbbing pain✅ Rinse with warm salt water • Call now
2. Knocked-out tooth (adult or child)✅ Keep in milk or saliva • See dentist in <60 min
3. Abscess or facial swelling with fever✅ Do not pop • Seek care immediately
4. Broken, chipped, or fractured tooth✅ Save fragments • Rinse gently
5. Lost crown, filling, or veneer✅ Temporary cement or sugar-free gum • Book same-day
6. Uncontrolled bleeding after extraction✅ Bite on clean gauze 30–45 min • Call provider
7. Pediatric emergency (child tooth trauma)✅ Stay calm • Save tooth • Pediatric specialist ready
8. Jaw injury, dislocation, or facial trauma✅ Ice 10 min on / 10 min off • ER if breathing issue
9. Object stuck between teeth or gum✅ Floss gently • Do not force • Professional removal
10. Loose implant or bridge✅ Avoid chewing on side • Implant specialist needed
